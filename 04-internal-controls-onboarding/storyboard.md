# Storyboard — Internal Controls & Audit Basics

| Screen | Objective Served | On-Screen Text | Visual | Interaction |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Hook** | Relevance framing | "Welcome to the team. In your first week, you'll hear words like 'control,' 'evidence,' and 'finding' a lot. Let's make sure they actually mean something to you by Friday." | Friendly welcome graphic | "Let's go" button |
| **2. Core Concept: What's a Control?** | Objective 1 | "A control is just a safeguard — a rule or process that prevents or catches a problem. Example: when someone leaves the company, IT is supposed to shut off their system access within 24 hours. That rule is a control." | Simple before/after icon (employee leaves → access removed) | "Next" button |
| **3. The Audit Cycle** | Objective 2 | "Auditing that control means walking through 4 steps. Tap each to see what happens using our example." | 4-step reveal: Control Identified → Evidence Requested → Evidence Reviewed → Finding Documented | Click-to-reveal detail per step, using the terminated-employee example throughout |
| **4. Findings vs. Recommendations** | Objective 3 | "If the evidence shows the control didn't work, that's a **finding**. What you suggest to fix it is a **recommendation**. They're not the same thing." | Two side-by-side cards | "Next" button |
| **5. Knowledge Check** | Objective 4 | Scenario: "You're checking whether terminated employees lose access within 24 hours. Which of these counts as acceptable evidence?" | Multiple choice: (a) A manager's verbal confirmation (b) A system log showing the exact access-removal timestamp (c) An email saying "it's handled" | Select → feedback explaining why a timestamped system log is the only verifiable evidence |
| **6. Completion** | — | Reaction question | Completion icon | Yes/No buttons |
