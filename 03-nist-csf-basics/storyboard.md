# Storyboard — NIST CSF Basics

| Screen | Objective Served | On-Screen Text | Visual | Interaction |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Hook** | Relevance framing | "Your department gets a new laptop-tracking spreadsheet, a phishing test, and an incident report template this quarter — all part of the same framework. Which one?" | Three simple icons | "Guess" button — no wrong answer, primes curiosity |
| **2. Core Concept: The Five Functions** | Objective 1 | "The NIST Cybersecurity Framework organizes everything into five functions. Tap each to see its plain-language meaning." | 5-card grid: Identify 🔍 / Protect 🛡️ / Detect 👀 / Respond 🚨 / Recover 🔄 | Click-to-reveal one-line definition per card; "Next" enabled after all 5 viewed |
| **3. Match the Scenario** | Objective 2 & 3 | "Each of these belongs to one function. Tap a scenario to see which one — and why your department is involved, not just IT." | 4 scenario cards: laptop inventory list (Identify), locking a shared drive (Protect), a strange login alert (Detect), reporting a lost device (Respond) | Click-to-reveal matched function + one-line "why this is your department's job too" |
| **4. Recap** | Reinforce Objective 1 | "Identify what you have → Protect it → Detect problems → Respond when they happen → Recover afterward." | Five icons shown left-to-right as a simple flow | "Next" button |
| **5. Knowledge Check** | Objective 4 | Scenario: "An employee reports their laptop was stolen from their car. Which function does reporting this belong to?" | Multiple choice: Identify / Respond / Recover | Select → feedback explaining Respond is the immediate action, Recover comes after |
| **6. Completion** | — | Reaction question | Completion icon | Yes/No buttons |
