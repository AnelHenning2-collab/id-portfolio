NIST Cybersecurity Framework Basics

Quick Question

Your department gets a new laptop-tracking spreadsheet, a phishing test, and an incident report template this quarter — all part of the same framework.

Which one is it part of?

The Five Functions

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework organizes everything into five functions. Tap each to see its plain-language meaning.

🔍Identify
Know what devices, data, and systems you have.
🛡️Protect
Put safeguards in place before something goes wrong.
👀Detect
Notice when something unusual is happening.
🚨Respond
Take action right away when a problem is found.
🔄Recover
Get back to normal and learn from what happened.

Tap all five to continue.

Match the Scenario

Each of these belongs to one function — and your department is involved, not just IT. Tap a scenario to see which one.

📋 Laptop inventory list
Identify. You can't protect what you don't know you have.
🔒 Locking a shared drive
Protect. A safeguard put in place ahead of time.
⚠️ A strange login alert
Detect. Noticing something unusual is happening.
📞 Reporting a lost device
Respond. Taking action right away.

  • Protect it
  • Detect problems
