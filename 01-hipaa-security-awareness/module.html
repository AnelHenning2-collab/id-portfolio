The Three Safeguards

HIPAA requires three types of safeguards to protect patient information. Tap each one to see a real example — that front-desk scenario was a Physical Safeguard issue.

📋
Administrative
Policies, training, and designating a Privacy Officer.
🔒
Physical
Locked file rooms, privacy screens, locking your workstation when you step away.
💻
Technical
Passwords, encryption, automatic screen lock timers.

Everyday Risk Scenarios

These four situations happen in almost every practice. Tap each card to see why it's a risk.

🖥️ Unlocked screen
Anyone walking by can view open patient records. Lock your screen (Win+L) any time you step away, even for a minute.
🗣️ Hallway conversation
Discussing a patient's condition where others can overhear is a Privacy Rule violation, even without using their name.
📱 Texting patient details
Standard text messages aren't encrypted. Use the practice's secure messaging system instead.
📧 Suspicious "verification" email
Phishing emails often impersonate insurers to steal login credentials. Verify by phone before clicking any link.

If You Suspect a Breach

If you think patient information may have been exposed — even if you're not sure — the first step is always the same:

  • Notice something that seems wrong
  • Tell your Privacy Officer immediately
  • The Officer investigates and handles next steps

You will not be punished for reporting a possible mistake. Reporting early is always the right call.
