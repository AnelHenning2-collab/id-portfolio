# Storyboard — CAC & LTV Fundamentals

| Screen | Objective Served | On-Screen Text | Visual | Interaction |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Hook** | Relevance framing | "A local coffee shop spends $200 on Instagram ads and gets 10 new customers. Did that work out?" | Simple ad mockup icon | Buttons: "Sounds good" / "Not enough info" — primes the idea that cost alone isn't the full picture |
| **2. Core Concept: CAC** | Objective 1 | "That's Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) — what you spend, on average, to gain one new customer. $200 ÷ 10 customers = **$20 CAC**." | Formula shown building piece by piece | "Next" button |
| **3. Live Calculator** | Objective 2 | "Try it yourself. Enter a campaign's spend and new customers to calculate CAC." | Two number inputs (Spend, New Customers) + Calculate button | Learner enters their own numbers, sees live CAC result computed and displayed |
| **4. Core Concept: LTV & the Ratio** | Objective 3 | "CAC alone doesn't tell you if $20 is a good deal. You also need Lifetime Value (LTV) — what a customer is worth over time. A healthy business generally wants LTV to be **3x or more** than CAC." | Two stat boxes side by side: CAC $20 vs LTV example $80 (4:1 ratio, shown as healthy) | "Next" button |
| **5. Knowledge Check** | Objective 4 (applied) | Scenario: "A campaign has a CAC of $50 and an LTV of $60. Is this campaign healthy?" | Multiple choice | Select answer → feedback explaining the ~1.2:1 ratio is too thin, not the 3:1 target |
| **6. Completion** | — | Reaction question | Completion icon | Yes/No buttons |
